Cortex Connections Overview

Cortex Connections is a neuroscience project aimed at exploring the differences in brain connectivity between individuals with ADHD and neurotypical individuals. Using advanced neuroimaging techniques and machine learning algorithms, I analyze brain activity to uncover unique patterns and insights.

Project Goals

  • Visualize and compare brain connectivity in ADHD and neurotypical individuals.
  • Identify potential biomarkers for ADHD.
  • Enhance our understanding of brain function and connectivity.

Methodology

I utilize fMRI data and employ techniques such as dictionary learning and seed-based GLM to analyze brain activity. My process involves:

  1. Collecting and preprocessing fMRI data from participants.
  2. Applying advanced neuroimaging techniques to extract meaningful patterns.
  3. Comparing and contrasting the results to draw significant conclusions.

Significance

The findings from Cortex Connections stem from a desire to contribute to the field of neuroscience, offering deeper insights into the neural mechanisms underlying ADHD. I am hopeful that explorations of this kind may inform future treatments and interventions, improving the lives of those affected by ADHD.
